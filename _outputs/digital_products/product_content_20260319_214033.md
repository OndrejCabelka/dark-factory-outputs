## Category 1: Listing Titles & SEO
*Prompts 1–9*

---

**Prompt #1: Complete Listing Title Generator**
```
I sell [PRODUCT NAME] on Etsy. My product is [2-3 sentence description of the product, including material, style, and who it's for]. My target buyer is [describe your ideal customer — age, occasion, aesthetic preference]. Generate 5 different Etsy listing title options for this product. Each title must be exactly 140 characters or fewer, front-load the most important keyword, use natural-sounding keyword phrases buyers actually search, avoid keyword stuffing, and separate phrases with either a comma, dash, or pipe symbol. After each title, explain in one sentence why you placed the primary keyword where you did.
```
*When to use: Every time you create a new listing and need a keyword-rich title that actually reads like a human wrote it.*

---

**Prompt #2: All 13 Etsy Tags At Once**
```
I need all 13 Etsy tags for my listing. My product is [PRODUCT NAME AND DESCRIPTION]. My primary buyer searches Etsy for [describe the occasion, problem, or desire that drives them to search — e.g., "a personalized gift for a new mom" or "rustic wedding decor"]. My shop style/aesthetic is [e.g., boho, minimalist, cottagecore, maximalist]. Generate exactly 13 Etsy tags. Each tag must be a multi-word phrase (2-4 words preferred), reflect real buyer search behavior, avoid repeating the same word across too many tags, and cover a mix of: product type tags, occasion tags, style/aesthetic tags, recipient tags, and material/technique tags. Present them as a numbered list with a one-line explanation of the search intent behind each tag.
```
*When to use: When setting up a new listing or auditing an existing one that isn't getting impressions.*

---

**Prompt #3: Competitor Title Analysis & Improvement**
```
Here are 5 Etsy listing titles from my top competitors selling [PRODUCT TYPE]: [PASTE COMPETITOR TITLES HERE, numbered 1-5]. Analyze these titles for: (1) which keywords appear most frequently, (2) any keyword gaps or opportunities none of them are targeting, (3) structural patterns they use, and (4) weaknesses I could exploit. Then write me 3 improved listing titles for my version of this product, which is [describe what makes your product different — material, personalization, style, price point, quality]. My titles should target any keyword gaps you identified while still including the highest-frequency terms my competitors are using.
```
*When to use: Before launching a product in a competitive niche to find the white space in search results.*

---

**Prompt #4: Underperforming Listing Title Refresh**
```
My Etsy listing is underperforming. The current title is: [PASTE CURRENT TITLE]. In the last 30 days, it has received [NUMBER] views and [NUMBER] sales. The product is [describe product in detail — material, size, use case, personalization options]. My current tags are: [PASTE ALL 13 CURRENT TAGS]. Diagnose what is likely wrong with this title from an SEO and click-through perspective. Then rewrite the title using better keyword placement and buyer-intent language. Also identify which 3-5 of my current tags should be replaced and suggest the replacements, explaining why each replacement improves my search visibility.
```
*When to use: When a listing has been live for 60+ days with low views and you need to diagnose and fix it.*

---

**Prompt #5: Long-Tail Keyword Research for a Niche**
```
I sell [PRODUCT TYPE] on Etsy. My niche is [describe your specific niche — e.g., "personalized jewelry for pet owners" or "printable planners for teachers"]. I need long-tail keyword phrases that buyers at the bottom of the purchase funnel use when they are ready to buy — not general browsing terms. Generate 25 long-tail keyword phrases (3-6 words each) that real buyers would type into Etsy search. Organize them into 5 groups: (1) occasion-based keywords, (2) recipient-based keywords, (3) style/aesthetic keywords, (4) material/format keywords, and (5) problem-solving keywords. Flag the 5 keywords across all groups that you believe have the best balance of search volume and lower competition for a smaller Etsy shop.
```
*When to use: When entering a new niche or planning a batch of new listings and need a keyword bank to pull from.*

---

**Prompt #6: Seasonal Keyword Upgrade**
```
I have an Etsy listing for [PRODUCT NAME] that I want to optimize for [UPCOMING SEASON OR HOLIDAY — e.g., "Christmas 2024," "Valentine's Day," "Back to School season"]. The current listing title is: [PASTE CURRENT TITLE]. My current tags are: [PASTE TAGS]. The holiday or season I'm targeting is [NUMBER] weeks away. Rewrite the listing title to incorporate seasonal buyer intent without making it sound like a throwaway seasonal product — it should appeal to both seasonal shoppers AND year-round buyers. Provide 5 new seasonal tags to add, and tell me which 5 of my current tags to temporarily swap out for the season and when exactly I should swap them back.
```
*When to use: 8–10 weeks before a major holiday or season when you need to pivot existing listings without rebuilding them.*

---

**Prompt #7: Digital Download SEO Title Pack**
```
I sell digital download products on Etsy. My specific product is [DESCRIBE THE DIGITAL PRODUCT — e.g., "a printable meal planning bundle with 5 PDF pages in a neutral aesthetic"]. The buyer who purchases this is typically [describe buyer — their job, hobby, life stage, or problem they're solving]. Generate 5 Etsy listing title options specifically optimized for digital download search behavior. Each title must include the word "printable," "digital," "instant download," or "template" where it fits naturally, front-load what the product actually IS, and include at least one use-case keyword. After all 5 titles, list the top 13 tags for this digital product with a note next to any tag that is specifically high-performing for digital/printable products on Etsy.
```
*When to use: When listing any digital product and you want titles that capture buyers who specifically filter for or search for digital downloads.*

---

**Prompt #8: Multi-Variation Listing Title Strategy**
```
I have an Etsy listing with multiple variations. The product is [PRODUCT NAME] and the variations include [LIST ALL VARIATIONS — e.g., "sizes: 8x10, 11x14, 16x20" or "colors: black, white, navy, blush" or "personalization: with name, without name"]. The problem I'm facing is that my single title can't capture searchers looking for specific variations. Write me one master listing title that covers the broadest buyer intent for this product. Then create a strategy document explaining: (1) which variation keywords to prioritize in my title vs. tags, (2) how to mention variations in my title without it sounding spammy, and (3) whether I should consider splitting this into multiple listings and under what conditions that makes sense for this specific product.
```
*When to use: When you have a product with many variations and you're unsure how to capture keyword traffic for all of them in one listing.*

---

**Prompt #9: Shop-Wide SEO Audit Prompt**
```
I'm going to paste the titles of [NUMBER] listings from my Etsy shop. Please analyze them as a group and identify: (1) which keywords I'm over-relying on across too many listings, which could cause my own listings to compete against each other; (2) keyword gaps — profitable search terms my shop appears to be missing entirely; (3) titles that are structurally weak and need prioritization for rewriting; and (4) whether my listing titles as a group tell a coherent brand story or feel scattered. Here are my listing titles: [PASTE ALL LISTING TITLES, one per line]. After your analysis, give me a prioritized action list: which 3 listings should I rewrite first, and give me draft rewritten titles for those 3.
```
*When to use: During a quarterly shop audit when you want to treat your listings as a portfolio rather than individual pages.*

## Category 2: Product Descriptions
*Prompts 10–18*

---

**Prompt #10: Physical Product Description — Full Build**
```
Write an Etsy product description for my physical product. Here are the details: Product name: [NAME]. Materials: [LIST ALL MATERIALS]. Dimensions/sizes available: [SIZES]. Colors/finishes available: [OPTIONS]. How it's made: [HANDMADE, PRINTED, CAST, ETC.]. Personalization options: [DESCRIBE OR WRITE "NONE"]. Who buys this: [DESCRIBE TARGET BUYER]. What occasion or need drives the purchase: [E.G., "A gift for a friend's birthday" or "home decor for a nursery"]. Write a description that opens with a scene or emotion (not a product feature), follows with benefits before features, includes a clear personalization/ordering instruction section if applicable, addresses the top 3 questions a buyer would have before purchasing, and closes with a warm call to action. Format with short paragraphs and use line breaks for readability. Do not use bullet points in the first two paragraphs.
```
*When to use: When building any new physical product listing from scratch and you want a conversion-focused description, not just a product spec sheet.*

---

**Prompt #11: Digital Download Description — Full Build**
```
Write a complete Etsy product description for a digital download product. Product details: What it is: [DESCRIBE THE PRODUCT]. File format(s): [E.G., PDF, PNG, SVG, XLSX]. Number of files included: [NUMBER]. Page/sheet count: [NUMBER]. Software needed to use it: [E.G., "No software needed — print at home" or "Editable in Canva/Adobe"]. Who it's for: [DESCRIBE BUYER]. Problem it solves or outcome it creates: [DESCRIBE]. The description must: open by speaking directly to the buyer's frustration or desire, clearly explain what they get and how they access it immediately after purchase, address the top buyer concern for digital products (which is "will this actually work for me?"), include a "What's Included" section in scannable format, explain the no-refund policy for digital goods in a warm, non-defensive tone, and end with encouragement for the buyer to reach out with questions.
```
*When to use: For any digital download listing — printables, templates, SVGs, spreadsheets, guides, or presets.*

---

**Prompt #12: Feature-to-Benefit Conversion**
```
I have written a product description for my Etsy listing but it reads like a spec sheet — it lists features but doesn't tell buyers why those features matter to them. Here is my current description: [PASTE YOUR FULL CURRENT DESCRIPTION]. My target buyer is [DESCRIBE BUYER IN DETAIL]. Rewrite this description by converting every feature into a buyer-focused benefit. For each feature you convert, use the internal logic: "This product has [FEATURE], which means you [BENEFIT/OUTCOME]." The rewritten description should make the buyer feel something — whether that's excited, relieved, confident, or understood. Keep all factual product information intact. Do not add claims you can't verify. Format the final version for Etsy's listing text field — short paragraphs, punchy sentences, scannable.
```
*When to use: When you know your product is good but your listing isn't converting and the description reads as flat or clinical.*

---

**Prompt #13: Gift-Angle Rewrite**
```
My Etsy listing currently describes this product as if the buyer is purchasing it for themselves. I want to rewrite the description to capture gift-givers — people buying this for someone else. My product is: [DESCRIBE PRODUCT]. The most common gift-giving occasions for this product are: [LIST 2-3 OCCASIONS — e.g., birthday, Mother's Day, wedding]. The recipient is typically: [DESCRIBE RECIPIENT]. Rewrite the description with gift-givers as the primary audience. The new description should: open with language that speaks to the feeling of giving a meaningful gift, describe the product through the lens of how the recipient will feel when they receive it, include specific occasion callouts naturally within the text, address gift-giver concerns (shipping time, packaging presentation, personalization deadlines), and end with a line about gift messaging or packaging if relevant. Also write me 3 alternate opening lines I can test.
```
*When to use: When you're approaching a gift-heavy season (holidays, Mother's Day, Valentine's) and want to capture buyers shopping for others, not themselves.*

---

**Prompt #14: Weak Listing Diagnosis**
```
My Etsy listing is getting views but not converting to sales. I need you to diagnose the description. Here is my full listing description: [PASTE DESCRIPTION]. My product price is [PRICE]. My product is [PRODUCT NAME AND BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. My main competitor's price range is [PRICE RANGE]. Review this description and identify: (1) the exact moment a buyer would likely stop reading and why, (2) any trust signals that are missing (guarantees, social proof mentions, material quality statements), (3) features being described without their corresponding buyer benefit, (4) anything in the description that could create doubt or hesitation, and (5) whether the call to action is clear and compelling. After your diagnosis, rewrite the three weakest sections of the description.
```
*When to use: When a listing has a decent click-through rate but a low conversion rate — people are visiting but not buying.*

**Prompt #15: The Sensory Description Builder**
```
I want to write a product description that makes my buyer feel like they can almost touch, smell, or experience my product through the screen. My product is [PRODUCT NAME]. It is made of [MATERIALS]. It looks like [DESCRIBE APPEARANCE IN DETAIL — texture, color, finish, weight]. When someone uses or displays this product, the experience is [DESCRIBE THE EXPERIENCE OF OWNING OR USING IT]. Write a product description that leads with deeply sensory, evocative language — textures, colors, weights, sounds, and feelings — while still including all necessary purchase information. The sensory language should appear primarily in the first two paragraphs. The remaining paragraphs should cover specifications, ordering instructions, and FAQs. This product appeals most to buyers who value [E.G., craftsmanship, natural materials, nostalgia, luxury, handmade quality].
```
*When to use: For handmade, artisan, or premium-priced products where the physical experience of the product IS the selling point.*

**Prompt #16: Bundle or Collection Description**
```
I'm listing a bundle or collection of products together on Etsy. Here's what the bundle includes: [LIST EACH ITEM IN THE BUNDLE WITH A BRIEF DESCRIPTION]. The bundle is priced at [PRICE]. Each item purchased separately would cost [TOTAL INDIVIDUAL PRICE]. My target buyer for this bundle is [DESCRIBE BUYER]. The main reason someone would buy this bundle instead of individual items is [DESCRIBE THE VALUE PROPOSITION — convenience, savings, cohesive aesthetic, complete solution, etc.]. Write a product description that: (1) opens by selling the complete outcome or transformation the buyer gets from owning the whole bundle, (2) lists each included item with its individual benefit in a scannable format, (3) clearly communicates the savings or added value vs. buying separately, (4) explains any variations or customization options that apply to the full bundle, and (5) addresses how the bundle will be packaged and shipped.
```
*When to use: When listing product bundles, gift sets, or curated collections and you want buyers to immediately understand why the bundle is the better choice.*

**Prompt #17: Description for a Made-to-Order Product**
```
I sell made-to-order products on Etsy, meaning each item is created after a customer places their order — nothing is premade. My product is [PRODUCT NAME AND DESCRIPTION]. The production time is [NUMBER] business days. Shipping time is [NUMBER] business days via [SHIPPING METHOD]. Total time from order to delivery is approximately [NUMBER] days. My target buyer is often someone who doesn't realize made-to-order means a longer wait time, which causes frustration. Write a product description that: proactively and warmly explains the made-to-order process without making it sound like a disadvantage, reframes the wait time as a signal of quality and craftsmanship, includes a clear timeline section buyers can refer to before ordering, addresses rush order requests (write [YES, I OFFER RUSH / NO, I DO NOT] here), and sets accurate expectations that lead to 5-star reviews rather than disappointed customers.
```
*When to use: For any handmade or custom product where production time is a key factor in buyer satisfaction and review outcomes.*

**Prompt #18: Personalized/Custom Product Description**
```
I sell personalized or custom products on Etsy. My product is [PRODUCT NAME]. Here is what buyers can personalize: [LIST ALL PERSONALIZATION OPTIONS — names, dates, colors, text, photos, etc.]. Here is exactly what I need from the buyer at checkout to make their item: [LIST EVERYTHING — e.g., "Name to be engraved," "Font choice from options A/B/C," "Date in MM/DD/YYYY format"]. Common mistakes buyers make when ordering: [LIST 1-3 COMMON ERRORS — e.g., "Forgetting to leave their personalization details in the notes," "Spelling errors in names"]. Write a product description that: clearly sells the emotional value of a personalized item in the opening, makes the ordering/customization instructions impossible to miss without being bossy, uses formatting to highlight exactly what the buyer must provide at checkout, reduces the #1 source of customer service messages I receive, and reminds buyers that personalized items cannot be returned due to their custom nature (write this warmly, not defensively).
```
*When to use: For any custom or personalized product where unclear instructions lead to delays, mistakes, and poor reviews.*

## Category 3: Shop Copy & Branding
*Prompts 19–25*

---

**Prompt #19: Etsy About Section — Full Write**
```
Write my Etsy shop "About" section. Here is my information: My name: [NAME]. My shop name: [SHOP NAME]. What I sell: [DESCRIBE YOUR PRODUCTS]. How long I've been selling/making: [TIMEFRAME]. My background before Etsy (if relevant): [BRIEF BACKGROUND OR WRITE "N/A"]. Why I started this shop: [YOUR REAL REASON — be honest]. Where I work/create: [DESCRIBE YOUR WORKSPACE OR LOCATION GENERALLY]. What makes my products or process different: [YOUR DIFFERENTIATOR]. My values as a maker/seller: [2-3 VALUES — e.g., sustainability, quality materials, fast shipping, supporting small business]. Write a 3-4 paragraph About section that sounds like a real, warm human being wrote it — not a corporate bio. It should make a buyer feel connected to me personally, trust my craftsmanship, and understand why buying from my small shop matters. Avoid clichés like "passion for" and "journey." End with one line that invites the buyer to look around the shop.
```
*When to use: When setting up your shop for the first time or when your current About section is a copy-paste of your social media bio.*

**Prompt #20: Shop Announcement — Seasonal/Evergreen**
```
Write two versions of my Etsy shop announcement. My shop name is [SHOP NAME] and I sell [PRODUCT TYPE]. Version 1 — Seasonal: I am currently approaching [UPCOMING HOLIDAY/SEASON]. My order cutoff date for guaranteed delivery by [DATE] is [CUTOFF DATE]. I want to communicate urgency without being pushy, highlight my most popular product for this season ([PRODUCT NAME]), and remind buyers about any current promotion ([PROMO OR WRITE "NO CURRENT PROMO"]). Version 2 — Evergreen: Write a shop announcement that works year-round, communicates my current production time of [NUMBER] business days, mentions my [FREE SHIPPING THRESHOLD OR POLICY], highlights that I welcome custom requests, and ends with a line that makes a first-time visitor feel welcomed. Both versions should be under 300 words and scannable — buyers rarely read long announcements.
```
*When to use: At the start of each season to refresh your shop announcement, and once to create your go-to evergreen fallback.*

**Prompt #21: Brand Voice Guide**
```
I need to define my Etsy shop's brand voice so that all my copy — listings, emails, social media, and shop pages — sounds consistent and recognizable. My shop name is [SHOP NAME]. I sell [PRODUCT TYPE]. My target buyer is [DESCRIBE IN DETAIL — demographics, lifestyle, values, aesthetics they love]. Three Etsy shops I admire for their copy tone (even outside my niche): [SHOP 1], [SHOP 2], [SHOP 3] — or describe tones you like if you don't have examples. Three adjectives I want buyers to use when describing how my brand "feels": [ADJECTIVE 1], [ADJECTIVE 2], [ADJECTIVE 3]. Three adjectives I absolutely do NOT want: [ADJECTIVE 1], [ADJECTIVE 2], [ADJECTIVE 3]. Create a one-page brand voice guide that includes: (1) voice adjectives with explanations, (2) do's and don'ts with examples, (3) sample phrases and banned phrases, (4) how my voice should shift slightly across different contexts (listing vs. email vs. Instagram), and (5) three sample sentences that perfectly capture my brand voice.
```
*When to use: Before writing any significant amount of shop copy, or when your shop has grown and you need consistency across multiple product lines.*

**Prompt #22: Shop Policies — Clear & Customer-Friendly**
```
Write my Etsy shop policies in language that is clear, professional, and warm — not cold, legal, or aggressive. Shipping: I ship from [COUNTRY/STATE], standard processing time is [NUMBER] business days, I ship via [CARRIER], and estimated delivery is [TIMEFRAME]. International shipping: [YES/NO — if yes, note any restrictions]. Damaged items: [YOUR POLICY]. Order cancellations: [YOUR POLICY AND TIMEFRAME]. Custom/personalization errors (buyer's fault): [YOUR POLICY]. Payment: [ACCEPTED METHODS]. Rewrite these policies in a format that: (1) sounds like a helpful human, not a terms-of-service document, (2) front-loads what the buyer wants to know most, (3) uses plain language a 14-year-old could understand, (4) still fully protects me as a seller, and (5) reduces "just checking in" messages by proactively answering common questions.
```
*When to use: When setting up your shop, after a policy-related dispute, or when your current policies are generating frequent customer confusion.*

**Prompt #24: Seller FAQ Section**
```
Write a Frequently Asked Questions section for my Etsy shop. Base the questions on the most common concerns buyers have, given these details about my shop: I sell [PRODUCT TYPE]. My products are [HANDMADE/PRINTED/DIGITAL/VINTAGE]. My typical price range is [PRICE RANGE]. My production time is [NUMBER] days. I do/do not [CIRCLE ONE] offer customization. I do/do not ship internationally. My biggest source of repeat customer service questions is [DESCRIBE YOUR #1 CUSTOMER SERVICE HEADACHE]. Write 10 FAQ entries in a Q&A format. Each question should sound like a real buyer typed it (use first-person buyer voice like "Can I...?" or "What if...?"). Each answer should be 2-4 sentences — complete and reassuring, never dismissive. The last FAQ should be: "I have a question that isn't answered here — what should I do?" with an answer that encourages messaging me directly with a warm, approachable tone.
```
*When to use: When adding an FAQ to your shop description, About page, or individual high-traffic listings to reduce repetitive customer messages.*

**Prompt #25: Rebranding Announcement Message**
```
My Etsy shop is undergoing a rebrand. I need to communicate this change to my existing customers and followers in a way that feels exciting — not confusing or alarming. Here's what's changing: Shop name change: [OLD NAME] → [NEW NAME] (or write "name staying the same"). ```
*When to use: Any time you pivot your shop focus, rename your shop, or significantly change your product line.*

**Prompt #31: TikTok/Reels Script — Etsy Seller POV**
